# Stage 6 prompt — Generate the three steps

For each of the four selected dreams, generate three concrete steps. Every step must be grounded in the research from Stage 5. Not generic advice. Specific, time-bound, and doable by the user without anyone's permission.

## The three steps

1. **Now** — something the user can do immediately, before they leave this session. Send an email, book a call, open a notebook and write the first page, make a phone call. The step must be completable in under 30 minutes, ideally under 10.
2. **Tomorrow before 11am** — the first step of the first day. The follow-up to the now step. Must be specific enough that the user can do it before coffee wears off.
3. **Day after tomorrow before 11am** — the second step. Proves the first wasn't a fluke. Builds the chain.

## Grounding rule

Every step must reference the research from Stage 5. If the research found that "Sarah learned Mandarin by booking a tutor on iTalki within 24 hours of deciding," the now step is "go to iTalki.com and book a trial lesson for this week." Not "find a language learning method." The step is grounded in a specific path that worked for a specific person.

If the research didn't surface a specific enough path, say so. Don't invent steps. Ask the user if they want to do more research or pick a different dream.

## What to refuse

Refuse steps that:
- Could apply to any dream ("set a goal," "make a plan," "do research")
- Have no verifiable completion ("start thinking about," "begin to explore")
- Require permission from someone the user hasn't identified ("ask your boss for a sabbatical" — unless the user named their boss in Stage 1)
- Are scheduled beyond the 48-hour window (the protocol is now, tomorrow, day after)

## The 11am deadline

Ferriss's specific protocol: the tomorrow and day-after steps happen before 11am. The reasoning: most people defer morning actions to evening, then skip them. 11am forces the action before the day's friction accumulates. The agent should name this rationale when the user asks why 11am.

## Pacing

5 minutes per dream. The agent suggests, the user edits. Don't over-engineer. The steps are starting points, not a master plan. The dreamline evolves.
