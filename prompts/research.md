# Stage 5 prompt — Find three people who've done it

This is the agentic value-add. The worksheet tells the user to do this; the agent does it for them. For each of the four selected dreams, find three people who've achieved it and surface their concrete steps.

## What to search for

For each dream, search for:
1. **First-person accounts** — blog posts, podcast transcripts, AMAs, Twitter threads where someone describes how they did the specific thing
2. **Interviews** — where someone is asked about their path to this specific outcome
3. **Documented processes** — case studies, course syllabi, documented routines

Prioritize first-person over third-person. "I learned Mandarin in 6 months, here's how" beats "How to learn Mandarin fast."

## Search strategy

Start broad, narrow fast:
- "[dream] how I did it"
- "[dream] my story"
- "[dream] blog post"
- "[dream] podcast transcript"
- "[dream] AMA"
- "[dream] reddit" (r/IAMA, r/AMA, niche subreddits)

Use the agent's web search and content extraction tools. Pull the concrete steps from the content, not the headline.

## What to surface

For each person found, surface:
- Name or handle
- Link to the source
- The 1-3 concrete steps they took, extracted from the content

Format:
```
[Person name/handle] — [source link]
- step 1: [what they did]
- step 2: [what they did]
- step 3: [what they did]
```

## What to skip

- Generic advice articles ("10 ways to learn a language")
- Listicles with no first-person account
- Marketing content for courses or coaching programs
- Anything where the "how I did it" is actually a sales pitch

## The bonus: the outreach script

For one of the three people found (the most accessible one), generate a short outreach script the user can send. Ferriss's protocol explicitly recommends asking someone who's done it for advice. The agent drafts the message so the friction is zero. The script:

- One sentence: what the user is trying to do
- One sentence: why this person specifically (reference their specific content)
- One ask: a 10-minute call or a single email reply with their top piece of advice
- One line of social proof or context on the user (optional)

Keep it under 100 words. The goal is a reply, not a meeting.

## Pacing

5-7 minutes per dream. Don't go deep on all three people per dream. Find them fast, extract the steps, move on. The research is for the user to dig into later. The agent's job is to surface the paths, not to write a biography.
